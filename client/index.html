Built on Atomic Habits

Your habits are votes for your identity

Instead of tracking a fragile consecutive-day count, Tally frames each habit as a vote cast toward the type of person you want to become. Three principles guide everything we built.

01

Define an identity, not a goal

Don't say "I want to read more." Say "I am a reader." Your habits flow from who you are, not a target on a checklist. Tally asks for your identity first — every habit you create is attached to it.

02

Cast a vote every time you show up

Every time you log a habit — even the scaled-down "I only did 1 page today" version — you cast a vote. Your total vote count and rolling consistency score are what grow. Nothing resets.

03

"Never miss twice" — the one rule

Missing one day is normal. If you miss two days in a row, Tally sends a gentle nudge — not a shame notification. The goal is to get back on track, not to guilt-trip you for being human.

Why Tally is different

Streak apps punish you. Tally doesn't.

Two common approaches to habit tracking both have a fundamental problem. Here's what we designed around instead.

Streak apps (e.g. Streaks)

A counter that can shatter

  • ✕ A single missed day resets everything to zero. 47 days of effort — gone.
  • ✕ The psychology is fragile: one bad day can feel like losing it all, so people quit entirely.
  • ✕ Tracks consecutive completion, not the identity you're building over time.
Gamified apps (e.g. Habitica)

Accountability through shame

  • ✕ A "boss" damages everyone in your party if you don't complete tasks. Social pressure via punishment.
  • ✕ Uses RPG health bars and damage mechanics — the visual language of failure, not growth.
  • ✕ Works through external fear, not internal identity-driven motivation.
Tally — vote-based identity tracking

Quiet, permanent accumulation

  • ✓ Votes only accumulate. A missed day dents your rolling consistency slightly — it never erases your history.
  • ✓ Scaled-down versions count. Couldn't run 5k today? "Walked around the block" still casts a vote for the identity "I move daily."
  • ✓ The nudge, not the punishment. Two missed days trigger a gentle check-in — nothing disappears, nobody in your group gets hurt.
  • ✓ Identity-first framing. Every habit you create is attached to "I am a ___." You build the person, not the score.

Optional, not required

Make it a family thing — if you want

Tally works completely on its own. But if you want an extra layer of accountability, invite a parent or sibling to your Family Group.

  • ✓ See a live feed of family members casting votes for their own identities — not a leaderboard, just a quiet reminder that the behaviour is normal.
  • ✓ Pick one person as your accountability buddy for a specific habit. They're notified only if you're about to miss two days in a row — not before.
  • ✓ No punishment mechanic. No one's health bar drops because you skipped the gym.
